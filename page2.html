History

The city of London was founded by the Romans and their rule extended from 43 AD to the fifth century AD, when the Empire fell. During the third century, Londinium, the name given to the town by the Romans, had a population of 50,000, mainly due to the influence of its major port. Main events in Londons history include:

  1. 1940 The Blitz begins
  2. 1948 London Olympics
  3. 1951 Festival of Britain
  4. 1965 Sir Winston Churchill's funeral
  5. 2012 Olympic and Paralympic Games
